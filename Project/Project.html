Project

Conceptual Brewery System Control

Overview

The West Peak Hotel - M/Monk Brewery

There are many ways to control the different steps in the brewing process. There is definitely an art which directly correlates with the science to control the brewing process.

My conceptualisation involves the control function and processes for small microbreweries/pilot plant constructions.

Utilising a Raspberry Pi for software application and the primary control module, whilst incorporating a series of Arduino’s for mechanical control functionality, which can also be controlled independently from the main control unit.

Conceptually building a small-scale brewing control system that will have the ability to run semi-autonomously and provide control and monitoring remotely.

Motivation

As I alluded to in my personal background section, my project idea relates to a current business endeavour of mine – A Micro Brewery, which will be constructed and run onsite through my Pub, the West Peak Hotel.

Specifically, my idea revolves in the technical element to the art of beer brewing. Currently hardware and software packages generally cater for large corporation style breweries, providing semi-automated systems, but still require a large amount of staff to control the process. This isn’t including the staff also required to maintain any of the mundane operation of the brewery either.

Craft brewers, according to the Brewers Association, are small, independent and traditional. They must meet requirements including less than 25% ownership by a large corporation or bigger brewer, and they must qualify for the Tax and Trade Bureau's excise tax differential by producing less than two million barrels annually.

Craft brewers are artisans and lovers of beer first and foremost. But craft brewing is also a manufacturing business with the attendant issues, such as expansion, quality, manufacturability and optimisation.

Falling well into the “Artisan” category we can’t fiscally commit to building a brand-new facility, with any of the high-end control systems and equipment required to properly take advantage of such a conceptually sound system.

Although scalable systems are on offer, these often require specifically fabricated mashing and fermenting systems.

According to a Deloitte statistical report - The production of craft beer in Australia has evolved from a small industry comprised of home-brew enthusiasts, to a large and rapidly growing industry with annual sales in excess of $370 million, growing at a rate of around 10% per year. Sales of craft beer represent an estimated 5%, and growing, of the total sales in the Australian beer market.

Detailed description of the features of the product or service

Designing and constructing a modular control system that offers us the flexibility to remotely monitor and control the multitude processes required to brew a decent beer, will be invaluable to our business.

Currently the 3 directors (myself and my two cousins) all work and reside in geographically different locations from the proposed microbrewery site. Brewing can be a simple process, but it can also become extremely complicated when trying to design a craft beer that will compete in the ever-increasing craft beer market.

The envisage the system to operate, manage, measure and test during several the phases in the brewing process. Functionality would include:

- Recipe system - Hosting a modifiable recipe database and software integration with some current 3rd party web-based brewing sites.
******* Hierarchical recipe system
Batch control centre software
******* Ingredient profiles
******* Gravity testing (Alcohol measurement)
******* Fermentable tables
******* Yeast characteristics
- Batch control - Fermenter control measures
******* Temperature profiling
******* wort control.
******* Water testing and ph. monitoring.
******* Nutrient testing and deficiency identification
******* Volume management
- Mechanical application and industrial vat/fermenter plumbing control
- Separation Procedures/Formulas
- Bluetooth/Wi-Fi functionality to remote measurement and control instruments
- Alerts (text / email) remote software access
- Ingredient phase control
Although there will always be a physical/manual labour requirement in the process. Having the ability to remotely monitor and control the brewing process, in particular the fermentation process where adjustments are often required on a daily basis.

Temperature control and wort chemical/nutrient balance is a priority during this phase and managing to monitor these elements will not only be a game changer for us personally, given our remote locality, but this may have some feasible application to any small scale brewers who can’t afford anything like the SIMATIC PCS 7 BOX ( Approx. $16k AU) and wish to analytically and systematically control their brewing process, maintaining the ability to keep the essence of the beer craft artisan profiling, which is currently so popular.

Software:

I don’t envisage the software development to be overly complicated as there is a fair amount of open source help available and the system architecture in essence, is rather simplistic.

3rd party assistance may be required when integrating certain componentry like current of the shelf measurement devices and Bluetooth/Wi-Fi compatibility.

My strengths are in hardware management, electronic/mechanical system design and construction. There may be some requirement to design and route specific PCB’s but this will be a phased process and I will endeavour to design around software application initially, giving me the required framework to complete this process.

Outcome

Ultimate success can be measured in control and application for my business which hopefully translates to a fiscal saving and increased profit margin through process.

Concept trademark and sale would also constitute a massive success, but this is ancillary to my ultimate goal which is feasible systematic process control, remote monitoring/management and functional variable measurement.

Ideally I design a real life working model over the coming years and develop a system that provides us access to brew a better beer.
